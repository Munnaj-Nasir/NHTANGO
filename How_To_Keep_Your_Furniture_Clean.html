How To Keep Your Furniture Clean

Furniture is one of the biggest investments in a home, and keeping it clean is key to making sure it lasts for years. Proper care not only helps your furniture look its best but also maintains its comfort and durability.

August 25, 2025

1. Dust Regularly
Dust can easily build up on wooden surfaces, shelves, and tables. Use a soft microfiber cloth to wipe down your furniture at least once a week. This prevents dirt from scratching the surface.

2. Vacuum Upholstery
Sofas and fabric chairs collect dust, crumbs, and pet hair. Use a vacuum with a brush attachment to clean upholstery thoroughly. For leather, use a damp cloth and a leather conditioner occasionally.

3. Use Protective Covers
If you have children or pets, protective covers can help save your furniture from spills and scratches. Slipcovers are easy to wash and replace when needed.

4. Handle Spills Quickly
Spills happen, but the quicker you clean them, the better. Blot liquids immediately with a clean cloth—never rub, as that can push the stain deeper. Use mild cleaners suitable for the fabric or wood type.

5. Avoid Direct Sunlight
Sunlight can fade fabric and damage wood finishes over time. Try to position your furniture away from windows or use curtains and blinds to limit exposure.

6. Rotate Cushions
To keep your sofa cushions from wearing out unevenly, rotate and flip them regularly. This keeps them firm and helps the fabric last longer.

By following these tips, you can extend the life of your furniture and keep your home looking clean, cozy, and welcoming.
